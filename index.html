About Me

Hello! I am Euihyeon Choi, currently a Ph.D. student in the Daniel Guggenheim School of Aerospace Engineering at the Georgia Institute of Technology. I am honored to be advised by Prof. Koki Ho within the Space Systems Optimization Group.

My research lies at the intersection of astrodynamics, systems engineering, and mathematical optimization. I am passionate about developing innovative, optimal solutions for complex aerospace challenges to enable sustainable and efficient space exploration.

My specific areas of interest include space logistics architecture optimization, on-orbit servicing, assembly, and manufacturing (OSAM), satellite constellation management, and multi-agent robotic routing for planetary exploration.

Before joining Georgia Tech, I gained practical experience as a researcher at the Aerospace Technology Research Institute within the Agency for Defense Development (ADD) in South Korea. I hold both a Master's and a Bachelor's degree in Aerospace Engineering from KAIST, where I also double-majored in Physics as an undergraduate.

Please feel free to explore my publications and projects using the tabs above. If you would like to discuss potential collaborations or shared research interests, I encourage you to reach out.
